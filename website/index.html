Cricket Player Dashboard

Established in 2018, the Windsor-Essex Cricket League (WECL) is dedicated to promoting cricket among youth, international students, and newcomers in the community. As the league grows, it aims to adopt data-driven innovations to enhance player selection and game competitiveness. Currently, team selection is mostly subjective, making it difficult to identify top-performing players. This dashboard presents a performance-based system for evaluating and selecting players using machine learning and player statistics from the 2024 season.

Player Rankings 2024

The player rankings were extracted directly from the official WECL website using web scraping with the pandas.read_html() function. These rankings provide the raw statistics used to build performance evaluations in batting, bowling, and fielding, serving as the analytical foundation of the project.

Final Cricket Levels

To evaluate individual strengths, players were categorized into six performance levels for batting, bowling, and fielding using median-based statistical analysis. This leveling system offers a structured, unbiased framework to benchmark and compare player capabilities.

Confidence Intervals

An XGBoost classifier was trained to predict the probability of each player fitting specific roles such as Batter, Bowler, Fielder, All-Rounder, or Wicketkeeper. These confidence scores provide an objective assessment of a player’s most likely role based on performance levels.

{{ confidence_intervals|safe }}

Dream 11 Team

The Dream 11 team was formed by selecting players with the highest confidence scores in each role, ensuring a balanced squad. This approach supports fair and strategic decision-making in team selection, aligning with WECL’s mission to embrace analytics for modern cricket management.
